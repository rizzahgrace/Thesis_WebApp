How weather affects solar output:
  • filter_dramaRainfall

    Rainfall affects solar power output drastically. Since rain is connected with cloud coverage, the solar panels receive little amount of sunlight. This affects the power production for the day. [1]
     
  • placeWind Speed

    According to multiple studies, wind speed has little to no correlation to solar power production. Although, it could affect dust accumulation in the panels ultimately affecting production. But hey it would be nice to know how fast the wind blows doesn't it? [1]
     
  • whatshotTemperature

    At higher values, temperature correlates directly with solar power output. If the temperature is high, it's hot, and more power is produced. [1]
     
  • filter_dramaSolar Irradiance

    Solar Irradiance is equivalent to solar intensity! It's the power received per unit area from the sun! [2]
     
  • placeWind Direction

    Much like wind speed, wind direction has little to no correlation to solar power production. Although, it could affect dust accumulation in the panels ultimately affecting power production. But hey it would be nice to know where the wind blows doesn't it? [1]
     
  • whatshotRelative Humidity

    Relative Humidity correlates negatively with solar power production! This means that if the day's humidity is low, solar production is high, and the inverse occurs if humidity is high.
     
  • filter_dramaOther Atmospheric Conditions

    There are other atmospheric conditions not covered in the graph that affects solar power output. One such is cloud coverage. Cloud coverage negatively affects solar power production, as it blocks out sunlight. This means that cloudy days equate to low production days.
